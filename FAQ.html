The basics
```
EarlyTube is a free web app that lets young children watch YouTube videos without any player controls — no rewind, no skip, no progress bar. A parent sets it up, locks it with a PIN, and hands it to the child. The video just plays forward. That's it.
It was built by a mom of a teenager on the autism spectrum. The rewind button became a loop he couldn't escape — the same second of video, repeated for hours. That behaviour is called stimming, and for some children it's reinforced by the very design of YouTube's player. EarlyTube removes the controls entirely, before the habit can form. You can read the full story on our about page.
Yes. Completely free. No ads, no paywalls, no hidden fees. If it helps your child and you want to support the project, there's a voluntary Ko-fi link — but there's absolutely no pressure and nothing is locked behind it.
No download needed. EarlyTube runs in any browser. On iPhone you can tap Share → Add to Home Screen to install it like an app. On Android, Chrome will prompt you to install it automatically.
```

The parent PIN
```
The default PIN is 1234. You should change it before giving the device to your child. Long press the EarlyTube logo on the home screen to open Settings, where you can set a new PIN.
If you're not signed in, the PIN is stored in your browser's local storage. On iPhone, Safari can clear this after a period of inactivity due to Apple's privacy settings. The fix is to sign in with Google — your PIN is then saved to your account and will never reset, even if you switch devices or browsers.
On the PIN entry screen, tap Forgot PIN? You'll be asked to verify your Google account, and your PIN will be reset to 1234. You can then set a new one in Settings. If you're not signed in, the PIN resets automatically to 1234 after a period of inactivity — so you can just wait, or clear your browser's site data for EarlyTube.
EarlyTube is designed for young children who don't know what a PIN is. Older children may figure out workarounds — they could close the browser tab, for example. It's not a security product, it's a habit prevention tool. It works best when used proactively, before a child learns to look for workarounds.
```
Watching videos
```
Either tap a video from the suggested list, or paste a YouTube link into the box. The video loads in a player. Tap play, then tap Lock the controls. An invisible overlay covers the player — your child can't interact with any controls. When you want to stop, tap Close and enter your PIN.
Yes. Sign in with Google and you'll see a personal video list section. Tap + Add to paste a YouTube link, give it a title, and optionally assign it to a category. Your list is saved to a Google Sheet in your own Google Drive — you can manage it there too.
Apple's iOS requires a user tap to start video playback — it can't be triggered automatically. So on iPhone, you see the video with a play button first. Once you tap play and then tap Lock the controls, a black strip covers the controls bar and an invisible overlay locks everything. Playback continues uninterrupted without reloading the video.
Tap the Suggest a fix button on that video. You can submit a replacement link and we'll update the list. You can also reach us directly at earlytubeapp@gmail.com.
```
Sign-in & account
```
No. The core app — curated videos, paste-a-link, PIN lock — works without any account. Sign-in is only needed if you want to save your own personal video list, or if you want your PIN to persist permanently across sessions.
It creates a Google Sheet in your Google Drive called "EarlyTube" where your personal video list is stored. It also saves your PIN to your account so it never resets. Your name and email are stored in our database only to identify your account — nothing else.
Make sure pop-ups are allowed for EarlyTube in your Safari settings. Go to Settings → Safari → Block Pop-ups and turn it off, or add EarlyTube as an exception. If the problem persists, try using Chrome on iPhone instead.
```
